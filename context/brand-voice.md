## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Earned Authority (not claimed)
We are a licensed Pennsylvania Commercial Registered Office Provider. The founder holds a PhD in Engineering Science, is a PA Notary Public, and an IRS Enrolled Agent (in progress). Don't say "trusted" — show credentials. Don't say "experienced" — cite the specific qualification.
- **Do**: "Licensed by the PA Department of State under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109."
- **Do**: "Founded by a PA-licensed notary and IRS Enrolled Agent."
- **Avoid**: "Trusted professionals," "experienced team," anything a national RA could also claim.

### 2. Deadline Urgency (without panic)
The 2027 dissolution deadline is real. Foreign entities that miss it CANNOT reinstate — they must re-register under a new name at full cost. Be direct about consequences. Never alarmist. Never vague.
- **Do**: "Foreign entities that miss the 2027 deadline cannot reinstate. They must re-register under a new name."
- **Do**: "636 days until the PA DOS deadline. Here's exactly what needs to happen."
- **Avoid**: "Your business may be at risk!" (vague), countdown timers with no context.

### 3. Local-First (Erie, PA address; Pennsylvania jurisdiction)
We physically receive mail at 924 W 23rd St, Erie, PA 16502. This is not a virtual mailbox or forwarding address — it is a real commercial office open during business hours. We know PA DOS procedures intimately because we live under them.
- **Do**: Reference PA-specific statutes (15 Pa. C.S. § 109), exact fees ($7 annual report, $5 change of registered office, $70 CROP filing), specific forms (DSCB:15-108).
- **Do**: Acknowledge that Erie is the venue county for all legal process received.
- **Avoid**: Generic national compliance language. "All 50 states" framing.

### 4. Regulatory Precision
This is a legal/compliance service. Vague language destroys trust. Use statute numbers, exact deadlines, specific fees, named forms.
- **Do**: "File DSCB:15-108 ($5 fee, processed 7–10 business days) to change your registered office."
- **Do**: "The PA Department of State began requiring annual reports in 2025 under Act 122 of 2022."
- **Avoid**: "Paperwork," "legal stuff," "filing requirements." Precision builds authority.

### 5. Zero-Pressure Conversion
CROP is annual infrastructure — not an emergency. Clients choose us because it's the smart decision, not because we scared them. Never pressure. Lead with education.
- **Do**: "Get protected" → "View plans" → "Start today"
- **Avoid**: "Act now," "limited time," "before it's too late" (unless specifically tied to the 2027 deadline with exact dates)

## Hook Types (mandatory — choose one per article)

The dynasty-seomachine `/write` command requires every article to open with ONE of these hook types. Never open with a definition.

| Hook Type | PA CROP Example |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Provocative Question** | "Does your PA LLC actually have a registered office — or just an address someone wrote down?" |
| **Specific Scenario** | "In October 2026, a Philadelphia process server tries to serve your lawsuit papers. Nobody's at your home address. That default judgment follows you for 10 years." |
| **Surprising Statistic** | "As of 2025, every one of Pennsylvania's 3.8 million registered business entities must file an annual report — and most owners don't know it." |
| **Bold Statement** | "Your home address on your PA LLC filing is a mistake you haven't been charged for yet." |
| **Counterintuitive Claim** | "The cheapest registered agent option could cost your Pennsylvania business its legal standing." |

## What Never to Write

- "Industry-leading" without a citation to back it up
- "Trusted by thousands" — we're early stage, don't overclaim
- "Easy as 1-2-3" — compliance is not trivial; respect the reader's intelligence
- "One-stop shop," "streamlined process," "seamless experience" — generic filler
- Opening a article with "What is a CROP? A CROP is a..." — banned by dynasty-seomachine `/write` command
- Any claim that Northwest Registered Agent, CT Corporation, or ZenBusiness could also make without modification

## Style Notes

- **Sentence length**: Vary. Short for impact. Longer for explanation. Never more than 3 long sentences in a row.
- **Paragraphs**: 2–4 sentences max. White space aids readability in compliance content.
- **Numbers**: Always spell out for 1–9. Use numerals for 10+. "$7 fee" not "seven dollar fee."
- **PA DOS references**: Link to `file.dos.pa.gov` when referencing filings.
- **Statute references**: Italicize or bold on first use: *15 Pa. C.S. § 109*
- **Dates and deadlines**: Always include year. "September 30, 2026" not "September 30."
