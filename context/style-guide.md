# PA CROP Services — Style Guide

## Formatting Rules

### Headers
- **H1**: One per page. Primary keyword. 50–70 characters.
- **H2**: Every 300–500 words. Include secondary keywords naturally.
- **H3**: Sub-sections within H2 blocks. No H4+ unless truly necessary.
- Never use bold headers instead of proper H-tags.

### Paragraphs
- Maximum 4 sentences per paragraph.
- Maximum 3 consecutive long paragraphs — break with a list, table, or callout.
- Use white space liberally. Compliance content is already dense.

### Lists
- Use bullet lists for 3+ parallel items.
- Use numbered lists for sequential steps only.
- Each list item: 1–2 sentences max. If longer, make it a sub-section.

### Tables
- Use for comparisons (pricing, entity types, deadlines, feature differences).
- Always include a header row.
- Keep to 3–5 columns for mobile readability.

## Language Rules

### PA-Specific Terminology (always use these exact terms)
| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| Commercial Registered Office Provider (CROP) | "registered agent" (for PA-specific content) |
| PA Department of State (PA DOS) | "Pennsylvania Secretary of State" (PA doesn't use this title) |
| Annual report | "annual filing," "yearly report" |
| Administrative dissolution | "business closure," "forced dissolution" |
| Service of process | "legal papers," "lawsuit documents" |
| DSCB (Department of State Corporation Bureau) forms | "state forms" |
| file.dos.pa.gov | "the PA DOS website" |

### Numbers and Fees (always be precise)
- Annual report fee: **$7** (for-profit entities)
- CROP filing fee: **$70** (Statement of Commercial Registered Office)
- Change of registered office: **$5** (DSCB:15-108)
- PA LLC formation: **$125**
- Pennsylvania statute: **15 Pa. C.S. § 109**

### Dates
- Always include year: "September 30, 2026" not "September 30"
- The critical deadline: "December 31, 2027"
- Year the requirement started: "2025" (annual report requirement effective 2025 under Act 122 of 2022)

### Abbreviations
- Spell out on first use: "Commercial Registered Office Provider (CROP)" — then use "CROP"
- PA DOS: spell out "PA Department of State" on first use, then "PA DOS"
- LLC, LP, PC: no periods, no explanation needed

## SEO Writing Patterns

### Article Introduction (required structure)
1. **Hook** (1–2 sentences): One of the 5 hook types from brand-voice.md. Never a definition.
2. **Agree** (1 sentence): Acknowledge what the reader already knows or feels.
3. **Promise** (1 sentence): Tell them exactly what they'll learn.
4. **Preview** (2–3 sentences): Brief overview of main sections.

### Call to Action Placement
- **Within first 300 words**: Link to `/compliance-check` ("check your PA compliance status")
- **Mid-article**: Link to relevant pricing or specific service
- **End of article**: CTA box linking to homepage or compliance check
- **Related articles**: 3–5 links to related PA CROP content

### CTA Box (at end of every article)
```html
<div class="cta-box">
  <h3>[Action-oriented headline]</h3>
  <p>[1–2 sentences connecting article topic to the service]</p>
  <a href="/compliance-check" class="cta-btn">Check Your PA Compliance Status →</a>
</div>
```

## What Makes a Good PA CROP Article

**Great signal:** Statute number cited with correct section  
**Great signal:** Specific PA DOS fee amount  
**Great signal:** Named form number (DSCB:15-108)  
**Great signal:** Actual PA DOS portal URL (file.dos.pa.gov)  
**Great signal:** Author credential mentioned naturally  
**Great signal:** Local Erie/Pennsylvania specificity  

**Weak signal:** Generic compliance advice applicable to any state  
**Weak signal:** Emotional language without factual basis  
**Weak signal:** Numbered lists of 10+ items that feel padded  
**Weak signal:** More than 2 consecutive paragraphs without a header
