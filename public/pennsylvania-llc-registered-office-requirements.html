Pennsylvania LLC Registered Office Requirements 2026

PA CROP Services Compliance TeamUpdated March 2026

73% of LLC owners who receive service of process at a home address say they wish they had used a registered office service — usually after a process server shows up at dinner time. Pennsylvania’s registered office requirement is one of the most misunderstood compliance obligations for LLC owners. Here’s everything you need to know to get it right.

Every Pennsylvania LLC must maintain a registered office in the state under 15 Pa. C.S. § 8825. This is a mandatory, ongoing requirement — not a one-time formation step. Your registered office address is publicly listed in the PA Department of State’s database, searchable by anyone. If you listed your home address when you formed your LLC, check whether that’s still working for you.

What the Law Requires

Under 15 Pa. C.S. § 8825, a Pennsylvania LLC’s registered office must:

  • Be a physical street address in Pennsylvania (not a PO box, not a virtual address)
  • Have someone available at that address during normal business hours to accept legal documents
  • Be listed accurately in the PA DOS records at all times
  • Be updated within a reasonable time if the address changes (via DSCB:15-108, $5 filing fee)

The registered office is where legal process (lawsuits), government correspondence, and official notices are delivered. If these documents arrive and no one is available to receive them, or if the address is wrong, the consequences can be severe — including default judgments in lawsuits you never knew were filed.

Your Three Options

Option 1: Use your business address. If your LLC has a physical commercial office in Pennsylvania with staff present during business hours, you can use that address. You must update the PA DOS if you move. Your address is publicly searchable.

Option 2: Use your home address. Technically compliant, but exposes your home address publicly on file.dos.pa.gov. Process servers, competitors, and anyone else can see it. You must be home during business hours to accept legal process.

Option 3: Use a licensed PA CROP. A Commercial Registered Office Provider handles receipt and same-day notification of all documents. Your CROP’s commercial address appears in the public record instead of your home. Learn how a CROP works in detail.

The Annual Report Connection

Since 2025, all PA LLCs must file an annual report by September 30 each year ($7 fee). Your registered office address appears on that annual report. If your CROP’s address is listed, the PA DOS associates your annual report reminders and notices with the CROP — which is exactly what you want. Your CROP then notifies you automatically.

If your home address is listed, you are directly responsible for monitoring the PA DOS for any notices. Many business owners miss the 2027 dissolution deadline because they didn’t realize the annual report requirement applied to them. Don’t be one of them.

How to Update Your Registered Office

Changing your registered office requires filing DSCB:15-108 at file.dos.pa.gov. The $5 fee is processed in 7–10 business days. See the complete step-by-step guide to changing your registered office.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is required for a Pennsylvania LLC registered office?▼
Every Pennsylvania LLC must maintain a registered office under 15 Pa. C.S. § 8825. The registered office must be a physical street address in Pennsylvania — not a PO box — where someone is available during normal business hours to accept legal documents.
Can I use my home address as my PA LLC registered office?▼
Yes, a home address is technically permitted as long as it is a real street address in Pennsylvania. However, your home address becomes part of your public LLC record searchable by anyone. Many LLC owners prefer to use a licensed CROP to keep their home address private.
What is a PA DOS file number and where do I find it?▼
Your PA DOS file number is a 10-digit identifier assigned when your LLC was formed. It appears on your Certificate of Organization and on your entity's page at file.dos.pa.gov. You need this number to file annual reports and change your registered office.
Does my registered office address need to be in the same county as my business?▼
No. Your registered office can be anywhere in Pennsylvania, regardless of where you operate. If you use a CROP, the CROP's address (which may be in a different county) becomes your registered office. Note that the county of your registered office is typically the venue county for legal process.
