Pennsylvania Annual Report Requirement 2026 — Complete Guide

PA CROP Services Compliance Team Updated March 2026
Key fact: As of 2025, every Pennsylvania business entity must file an annual report with the PA Department of State. The fee is $7. The deadline is September 30 each year. Starting in 2027, failure to file risks administrative dissolution.

In 2022, Pennsylvania passed Act 122 — a sweeping reform that, for the first time, required all registered business entities to file annual reports with the Department of State. The law took effect in 2025. If your Pennsylvania LLC, corporation, or partnership was formed before 2025, you may have gone years without this obligation. That changed.

This guide covers everything Pennsylvania business owners need to know: who must file, what the deadlines are, what the $7 fee covers, how to file, and what your Commercial Registered Office Provider (CROP) does to protect you from missing it.

Who Must File a PA Annual Report?

The Pennsylvania annual report requirement applies to virtually every registered business entity. Specifically, all domestic and foreign filing entities must file, including:

  • Limited Liability Companies (LLCs) — domestic and foreign
  • Business Corporations — domestic and foreign
  • Limited Partnerships (LPs)
  • Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs)
  • Professional Corporations and Professional Associations
  • Business Trusts

There are narrow exemptions for certain nonprofit entities and insurance companies regulated separately. If you are uncertain whether your entity must file, use our free compliance check tool or contact the PA DOS Bureau of Corporations directly.

What Information Is Required on the Annual Report?

The Pennsylvania annual report is intentionally simple. It asks for basic current information about your entity:

  • Entity name (as registered with PA DOS)
  • PA DOS file number
  • Registered office address — this is where your CROP address appears
  • Names and addresses of governors (officers/directors/managers)
  • Principal office address
  • Brief statement of business activity

Critically, the registered office address must be current and accurate. If you are using a CROP, ensure the address on your annual report matches the address your CROP has filed with the PA DOS. Discrepancies can create compliance complications.

How to File Your PA Annual Report

The PA DOS has moved annual report filings online. Follow these steps:

  1. Go to file.dos.pa.gov — the official PA Business Filing Services portal.
  2. Search for your entity by name or PA DOS file number (format: 0012345678).
  3. Select "Annual Report" from the filing options for your entity.
  4. Verify or update your information — registered office address, officer names, principal office.
  5. Pay the $7 fee by credit card. You will receive a confirmation number.
  6. Save your confirmation — this is your proof of filing. Keep it for your records.

The entire process takes 5–10 minutes for most entities. If your registered information is already current with the PA DOS, it is even faster.

The 2027 Deadline — Why This Year Matters

The ordinary September 30 deadline carries a $7 fee and a late filing risk. The 2027 deadline is categorically different. Under Act 122 of 2022, the PA DOS will begin administrative dissolution proceedings for entities that have not filed their annual reports by December 31, 2027.

For domestic entities, administrative dissolution means your LLC or corporation loses its right to do business in Pennsylvania. For foreign entities, the consequences are worse: foreign entities that are administratively terminated cannot reinstate — they must re-register under a new name at full formation cost. How a CROP Protects Your Annual Report Compliance

The PA Department of State does not mail postcards to individual business owners about annual report deadlines. Instead, they send notice data to CROPs, who are then responsible for notifying their clients. If you are using a national registered agent that does not operate as a licensed Pennsylvania CROP, you may not receive these notices at all.

What Happens If You Miss the Annual Report Deadline?

Missing the September 30 annual report deadline does not result in immediate dissolution in 2025 or 2026. The PA DOS enforcement timeline is as follows:

  • 2025 and 2026: Late filings accepted. No automatic penalties beyond any late fees.
  • December 31, 2027: Entities that have not filed at least one annual report face administrative dissolution or termination proceedings.
  • After dissolution: Domestic entities must file a reinstatement application and pay reinstatement fees. Foreign entities cannot reinstate — they must re-register under a new name.

The most important action you can take right now is to ensure your entity has filed its 2025 annual report (due September 30, 2025) and is set up to file on time in 2026 and beyond.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Pennsylvania annual report due?▼
PA annual reports are due September 30 each year for most entities. The first reports were due September 30, 2025. The $7 fee applies to for-profit domestic and foreign entities.
Who must file a PA annual report?▼
All domestic and foreign filing entities registered with the PA Department of State must file annual reports. This includes LLCs, corporations, limited partnerships, limited liability partnerships, and professional associations.
What happens if I miss the PA annual report deadline?▼
Failure to file by the deadline can result in administrative dissolution (domestic entities) or termination (foreign entities). Starting in 2027, the PA DOS will begin enforcement actions for entities that have not filed any annual reports.

Where do I file my PA annual report?▼
