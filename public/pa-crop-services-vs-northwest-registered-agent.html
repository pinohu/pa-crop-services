PA CROP Services vs Northwest Registered Agent

PA CROP Services Compliance TeamUpdated March 2026

Northwest Registered Agent is one of the most respected registered agent companies in the United States, known for privacy-focused service and a strong reputation among small business owners. They operate in all 50 states, charge $125/year for registered agent service, and include same-day document scanning as a standard feature. For multi-state businesses or those who value a proven national provider, Northwest is a legitimate option.

But Pennsylvania has a compliance structure that most national providers — including Northwest — were not built around. This page compares PA CROP Services and Northwest Registered Agent across the dimensions that matter most for Pennsylvania businesses: licensing, pricing, annual report handling, and 2027 deadline preparedness.

About Northwest Registered Agent

Northwest Registered Agent was founded in 1998 and is headquartered in Spokane, Washington. The company built its reputation on a privacy-first philosophy: they do not sell customer data to third parties and include a Corporate Guide program where customers get individual support rather than call center queues. Their $125/year price point is competitive among national providers and includes standard features like document scanning, a secure online dashboard, and a registered office address in each state where you need service.

Northwest is an especially popular choice for multi-state businesses that want a single vendor to manage registered agent service across several states. They also offer a free first year of registered agent service when you form a new LLC or corporation through their formation service. This makes them an attractive entry point for first-time business owners.

What Northwest does not offer is a Pennsylvania Commercial Registered Office Provider license. Under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109, any provider acting as the registered office for multiple Pennsylvania entities must be licensed by the PA Department of State. Northwest operates as a registered agent under the general framework rather than as a licensed PA CROP. This distinction is more than a label — it affects accountability, PA DOS reporting obligations, and the specific expertise the provider brings to Pennsylvania compliance events.

PA-Specific 2027 Deadline Sequencing

Act 122 of 2022 created mandatory annual report requirements effective January 1, 2025, with enforcement — meaning administrative dissolution and termination — beginning in 2027. The deadlines are entity-type-specific: June 30 for corporations, September 30 for LLCs, December 31 for limited partnerships and LLPs. Northwest's compliance system provides generic multi-state reminders. PA CROP Services has built PA-specific reminder sequences for each entity type at 90, 60, 30, 14, and 7 days before each deadline.

2027 Enforcement Note: For foreign entities registered in Pennsylvania, the stakes are especially high. Domestic entities that miss the annual report deadline face administrative dissolution, which is reversible. Foreign entities face administrative termination — a permanent status with no reinstatement path. The business name may then be taken by another entity. PA-specific expertise in tracking these deadlines is especially valuable for foreign entity clients. See PA foreign entity annual report requirements.

How to Switch From Northwest to PA CROP Services

Switching registered office providers in Pennsylvania involves two steps: notifying the PA DOS and canceling your existing service. The process takes approximately 7–10 business days and costs $5 in state filing fees.

  1. Sign up with PA CROP Services. Choose your plan at pacropservices.com/#pricing. Your new registered office address will be 924 W 23rd St, Erie, PA 16502.
  2. File DSCB:15-108 with the PA Department of State to change your registered office address. The filing fee is $5. You can file online through the PA DOS business filing portal at file.dos.pa.gov.
  3. Update downstream records. Notify your bank, IRS, and any state licenses that reference your registered office address. The registered office address appears on your PA entity registration record but does not automatically update external records.
  4. Cancel Northwest service after confirming the DSCB:15-108 has been processed (typically 7–10 business days). Do not cancel before the PA DOS confirms the new address to avoid a gap in registered office coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Northwest Registered Agent a licensed PA CROP?+
No. Northwest Registered Agent operates as a registered agent service in Pennsylvania but does not hold a PA CROP license under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109. They provide a compliant registered office address, but without the PA-specific licensing framework and accountability obligations that come with a CROP license.

Can I switch from Northwest to PA CROP Services?+

What is the PA CROP licensing requirement and does it matter?+
Under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109, providers that act as registered office for more than one entity must be licensed CROPs. The license signals PA DOS accountability and formal compliance obligations that unlicensed national providers do not carry. PA CROP Services holds license DOS #0015295203.
What happens if my registered office provider fails to handle DOS correspondence?+
If your registered office address fails to forward official PA DOS mail — including annual report reminders and dissolution notices — your entity can be administratively dissolved without your knowledge. Choosing a licensed PA CROP with a real PA address and redundant document handling reduces this risk significantly.

PA CROP Services has built PA-specific reminder sequences for the 2027 enforcement deadline — 90, 60, 30, 14, and 7-day notices keyed to each entity type's specific deadline: June 30 for corporations, September 30 for LLCs, December 31 for LPs and LLPs.
Can a foreign entity use either service for its PA registered office?+
Yes, both services can provide a Pennsylvania registered office for foreign entities.
