Pennsylvania Foreign Entity Annual Report Requirements — 2026 Guide

PA CROP Services Compliance TeamUpdated March 2026
Critical warning: Foreign entities that fail to comply with PA’s 2027 deadline face administrative termination with no reinstatement option. This is the most severe compliance risk in the Pennsylvania business registry.

Foreign entities are businesses formed in another state that are registered to conduct business in Pennsylvania. If you formed your LLC in Delaware, Wyoming, or any other state and then registered in Pennsylvania to operate here, you are a foreign entity. You have the same annual report obligations as domestic entities — with one critical, irreversible difference.

What Foreign Entities Must Do

Every foreign entity registered in Pennsylvania must file an annual report with the PA DOS by September 30 each year. The fee is $7. The same file.dos.pa.gov portal used by domestic entities handles foreign entity annual reports. The same information is required: registered office, officer names, principal office, business activity.

Foreign entities must also maintain a registered office in Pennsylvania — which means either a physical PA address or a licensed Pennsylvania CROP. Many foreign entities established their PA registered office years ago and have not reviewed whether it is still accurate.

The 2027 Catastrophic Risk for Foreign Entities

When the PA DOS begins administrative action against non-compliant entities in 2027, domestic and foreign entities face different outcomes. A domestic LLC can file for reinstatement after dissolution and continue operating under the same name. A foreign entity cannot.

Under the framework established by Act 122 of 2022, administratively terminated foreign entities must apply for a new Certificate of Authority to do business in Pennsylvania. This is not reinstatement — it is an entirely new registration. If another entity registered your Pennsylvania business name during the termination period, you may not be able to use that name again in Pennsylvania.

For businesses that have operated in Pennsylvania for years under a specific name — with contracts, bank accounts, client relationships, and branding tied to that name — this is a business-ending risk, not just an administrative inconvenience. Learn more about the full 2027 deadline implications.

Compliance Checklist for Foreign Entities in PA

  • ✓ Confirm your PA Certificate of Authority is active (search at file.dos.pa.gov)
  • ✓ Verify your registered office address is current and your CROP is licensed
  • ✓ File your 2025 PA annual report if you haven’t already ($7 at file.dos.pa.gov)
  • ✓ Set up automated reminders for September 30, 2026 filing
  • ✓ Confirm your home state registration is also current
  • ✓ Verify your PA business name has not been registered by another entity

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a foreign entity in Pennsylvania?▼
A foreign entity is a business formed in another state (or country) that has registered to conduct business in Pennsylvania by filing for a Certificate of Authority with the PA DOS. The entity remains incorporated in its home state but has a Pennsylvania filing obligation.
Do foreign entities pay the same annual report fee?▼
Yes. Foreign entities pay the same $7 annual report fee due September 30 each year. The difference is in the consequences of non-compliance — foreign entities face termination with no reinstatement option starting in 2027.
What does 'administratively terminated' mean for a foreign entity?▼
Administrative termination is the foreign entity equivalent of administrative dissolution for domestic entities. The PA DOS removes the entity's Pennsylvania registration. Unlike domestic dissolution, termination is permanent — the entity cannot reinstate and must apply for a new Certificate of Authority.
If my foreign entity is terminated, can I register again under the same name?▼
If your business name was not taken by another PA entity during the termination period, you may re-register under the same name. If the name was taken, you must use a different name for your PA registration, which can cause significant business disruption.
