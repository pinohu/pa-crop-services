How to Change Your Registered Office in Pennsylvania

✦ Ikechukwu P.N. Ohu, PhD · PA Notary PublicUpdated March 2026
Quick facts: Filing fee $5 • Form DSCB:15-108 • File at file.dos.pa.gov • Processing 7–10 business days

Changing your Pennsylvania registered office is one of the most straightforward state filings you will ever make. The process takes about 10 minutes online and costs $5. What most business owners don’t know is when to make the change, what happens to their mail during the transition, and why the address they list matters more than they realize.

This guide walks through every step of changing your registered office, whether you’re switching from a home address, a national registered agent, or a different PA CROP. When Should You Change Your Registered Office?

Several common situations call for a registered office change:

  • You listed your home address when forming your LLC and want to protect your privacy
  • You’re moving to a new address and need to update your PA DOS record
  • You’re dissatisfied with your current registered agent or CROP
  • Your current provider is going out of business or ceasing operations
  • You want PA-specific annual report reminders and support for the 2027 deadline

Step-by-Step: How to Change Your Registered Office in PA

  1. Choose your new registered office. If you are switching to a licensed CROP like PA CROP Services, sign up first so your new address is confirmed. The CROP’s address (924 W 23rd St, Erie, PA 16502 for PA CROP Services) will be what you enter on the form.
  2. Go to file.dos.pa.gov. Navigate to PA Business Filing Services and log in or create an account.
  3. Search for your entity. Enter your entity name or PA DOS file number (format: 0012345678).
  4. Select “Change Registered Office.” This is DSCB:15-108. Enter the new registered office address exactly as it will appear in your public record.
  5. Pay the $5 filing fee. Credit or debit card. You receive a confirmation number immediately.
  6. Wait for confirmation. Standard processing is 7–10 business days. You will receive a stamped copy by email or mail.
  7. Cancel your old service. After the PA DOS change is confirmed, notify your previous registered agent or CROP that you are ending service.
  8. Update your other records. Notify the IRS (Form 8822-B), PA DOR, your bank, and any contracts referencing your registered office address.

What Happens to Your Mail During the Transition?

During the 7–10 business day processing period, your registered office is still technically your old address in the PA DOS system. Your old provider should continue receiving and forwarding your mail during this window.

Once the PA DOS processes the change, all future official correspondence routes to your new registered office. There is typically a brief overlap period where some pieces may arrive at the old address. Ask your old provider to forward any mail received after your official change date.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to change your registered office in Pennsylvania?▼
The PA DOS filing fee is $5 for DSCB:15-108 (Statement of Change of Registered Office). There is no additional state fee.
How long does a registered office change take in Pennsylvania?▼
Standard processing at the PA DOS is 7-10 business days. You can request expedited processing for an additional fee.
Can I change my registered office online?▼
Yes. File DSCB:15-108 online at file.dos.pa.gov through PA Business Filing Services. You need your entity's DOS file number to complete the filing.
Do I need to notify anyone else when I change my registered office?▼
Yes. After the PA DOS change is confirmed, update your address with the IRS (Form 8822-B), PA Department of Revenue, your bank, and any partners or vendors who have your registered office address on file.
What if I'm switching from a national registered agent to PA CROP Services?▼
File DSCB:15-108 listing 924 W 23rd St, Erie, PA 16502 as your new registered office. After the PA DOS confirms the change, cancel your previous service. Your documents will begin arriving at PA CROP Services' address immediately after the change is recorded.
