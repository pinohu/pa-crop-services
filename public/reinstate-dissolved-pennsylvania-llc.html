How to Reinstate a Dissolved Pennsylvania LLC

PA CROP Services Compliance TeamUpdated March 2026
Foreign entity warning: Foreign entities (formed in another state) that are administratively terminated in Pennsylvania cannot reinstate. They must re-register from scratch, possibly under a different name. This guide covers domestic PA LLCs only.

Your Pennsylvania LLC has been administratively dissolved. That stark word — dissolved — appears next to your entity on the PA DOS public search. Before you panic, know this: domestic Pennsylvania LLCs can be reinstated. The process requires a specific application, payment of fees, and filing of any delinquent annual reports. Here’s exactly how to do it.

What Administrative Dissolution Means

Administrative dissolution is different from voluntary dissolution. You didn’t choose to close your LLC — the PA Department of State issued a dissolution after you failed to meet a compliance requirement, most commonly the annual report obligation that became effective in 2025.

When your LLC is administratively dissolved:

  • The status “Dissolved” appears publicly on file.dos.pa.gov
  • Your LLC technically loses the right to conduct business in Pennsylvania
  • Your liability protection may be compromised for acts taken during dissolution
  • Contracts entered during dissolution may be unenforceable
  • Your business bank account may be flagged or frozen

The good news: these effects are reversible for domestic entities. Reinstatement restores your LLC to good standing as if the dissolution had not occurred, with some limitations.

Step-by-Step: Reinstating Your PA LLC

  1. Verify the dissolution. Go to file.dos.pa.gov and search for your entity. Confirm it shows “Dissolved” status and note the dissolution date.
  2. File all delinquent annual reports. Before reinstatement, you must file every annual report that was due. Annual reports cost $7 each. If you missed 2025 and 2026, that’s $14 in additional fees.
  3. File the Reinstatement Application. At file.dos.pa.gov, locate your entity and submit the Application for Reinstatement. The fee is $70.
  4. Update your registered office. If your registered office is outdated or you want to switch to a CROP, this is the right time. File DSCB:15-108 alongside your reinstatement ($5 fee).
  5. Receive confirmation. Processing takes 7–10 business days. Your entity status will update from “Dissolved” to “Active” in the PA DOS system.
  6. Set up annual report compliance. After reinstatement, you must file annual reports by September 30 each year to stay in good standing. Set up a CROP or reminder system immediately. Read the complete annual report guide.

The most important thing after reinstatement is ensuring it never happens again. Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to reinstate a dissolved Pennsylvania LLC?▼
The reinstatement application fee is $70. You must also file and pay for all delinquent annual reports ($7 each). If your LLC has been dissolved for multiple years with unfiled reports, costs can reach $70 + ($7 × number of years).
How long does PA LLC reinstatement take?▼
Standard processing at the PA DOS takes 7-10 business days. Expedited processing is available for an additional fee.
Can I still operate my business while it is administratively dissolved?▼
Technically no. An administratively dissolved LLC has lost its right to conduct business in Pennsylvania. Contracts entered during dissolution may be unenforceable, and banks may freeze accounts.
What happens to my business name during dissolution?▼
During dissolution, your business name remains associated with your entity in the PA DOS records. However, it may become vulnerable to registration by another party if the dissolution is extended. Reinstatement before your name is taken is critical.
Can a foreign entity reinstate after administrative termination?▼
No. Foreign entities that are administratively terminated in Pennsylvania cannot reinstate. They must apply for a new certificate of authority as a new foreign registration, potentially under a different name if their original name has been taken.
