How to File Your Pennsylvania Annual Report 2026 — Step by Step

PA CROP Services Compliance TeamUpdated March 2026
Before you start: Have your PA DOS file number ready. You need it to file online. Find it at file.dos.pa.gov or on your original formation documents.

The PA DOS annual report portal is straightforward once you know where to go. Most filings take under 10 minutes. This guide walks through every screen, every field, and every common mistake — so you file correctly the first time and don’t worry about the 2027 dissolution consequences.

What You Need Before You Start

  • Your entity’s PA DOS file number (format: 0012345678)
  • Your registered office address (your CROP’s address if applicable)
  • Names and addresses of your current governors (officers, directors, or managers)
  • Your principal office address
  • A credit or debit card for the $7 fee

Step-by-Step Filing Instructions

  1. Go to file.dos.pa.gov. Click “Annual Report” or search for your entity under “Business Entity Search.”
  2. Find your entity. Enter your entity name or PA DOS file number. Click on your entity in the results.
  3. Select “File Annual Report.” This option appears when an annual report is due or overdue.
  4. Verify your registered office. Confirm the registered office address is current. If you recently changed to a CROP, make sure the new address is already reflected. If not, update it here.
  5. Update governor information. Confirm or update the names and addresses of your officers, directors, or managers. This information becomes part of your public record.
  6. Enter your principal office. This is where your business primarily operates. It can be different from your registered office.
  7. Describe your business activity. A brief phrase is sufficient: “Limited liability company providing consulting services” or “Real estate investment LLC.”
  8. Review and pay. Review all entered information, then pay the $7 fee by credit or debit card.
  9. Save your confirmation. You receive a confirmation number and a stamped filing confirmation. Save this — it is your proof of compliance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Wrong file number format. PA DOS file numbers are 10 digits. If yours is shorter, add leading zeros: “1234567” becomes “0001234567.”
  • Outdated registered office. If your home address is listed but you’ve moved, update it now. Using an incorrect registered office can result in missed legal notices.
  • Filing for the wrong entity. If you have multiple entities, confirm you are filing the correct one. Each entity files separately.
  • Missing the deadline. Annual reports are due September 30 each year. Late filings are accepted but don’t wait — the 2027 consequences apply to entities that have never filed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get my PA DOS file number?▼
Your PA DOS file number appears on your original Certificate of Organization or Articles of Incorporation. You can also find it by searching file.dos.pa.gov for your entity name.
What if my information on file with the PA DOS is wrong?▼
You can update your registered office, officer names, and principal office address as part of the annual report filing process. You may also need to file a separate amendment if significant information has changed.
Can I file my annual report by mail?▼
The PA DOS strongly prefers online filing. Paper filings are accepted but may take longer to process. Online filing at file.dos.pa.gov is faster and provides immediate confirmation.
What happens after I submit my annual report?▼
You receive a confirmation number immediately after online submission. The PA DOS processes the filing and your entity status updates to reflect the filed annual report. Keep the confirmation for your records.
