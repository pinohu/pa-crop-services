The scenario every PA accountant has lived through

It's April. A client calls: "The state is going to dissolve my LLC. Can you help?"

You're in the middle of tax season. Extensions, S-corp returns, IRS correspondence. Now a client you've been serving for four years — and whose PA filing history you had no formal responsibility for — is panicking about a Department of State notice.

You can either: (a) quote them a fee to handle it, which you don't really want to do because PA state filings aren't your core work and the margin doesn't justify the risk; or (b) tell them you don't do that, which makes you the wrong answer in a moment when they trusted you with the question.

The third option: refer them to someone who already handles this for dozens of PA businesses, who'll pick up within a business day, and who'll pay you recurring revenue for the connection. That's us. That's this partnership.

Common partner questions

Straight answers.

The ones that come up on almost every first call.

How does the referral fee structure work?

Two models. Model A: flat referral fee of 20% of first-year revenue, paid within 30 days of client signup. Model B: 15% recurring revenue share for the life of the client. Most CPAs choose Model B for the compounding effect. You pick when we set up the partnership.

Do you handle both LLCs and corporations?

Yes. All four plans cover any PA entity type — domestic and foreign LLCs, business corporations, nonprofits, limited partnerships, LLPs, and business trusts. Deadline tracking adjusts automatically (Corps: June 30, LLCs: Sep 30, LPs/LLPs/trusts: Dec 31).

What happens to my client relationship if I stop referring?

Nothing changes for clients you've already referred. They stay on their plans, we keep serving them, and your revenue share keeps paying as long as they're active (Model B) or you keep your flat fee from initial signup (Model A). We don't recruit your other clients.

Can you white-label under my firm's name?

Yes, with some constraints. The registered office listing on the PA DOS filing must show us as the CROP (that's a state requirement, not a partnership choice). But the client-facing portal, email communications, and invoicing can be branded to your firm. Ask us about Enterprise White-Label for firms with 10+ clients.

What about clients who need legal advice?

We don't give legal advice and we say so clearly to every client. If a referred client has a legal question — entity dispute, structural change, tax controversy — we refer them to you or to a PA-licensed attorney. We stay in the filing and compliance lane.

Is there an exclusivity requirement?

No. Refer clients when it fits. Don't when it doesn't. We'd rather earn the referral each time than lock you into a contract.
