What Is a Pennsylvania CROP? Everything You Need to Know

PA CROP Services Compliance TeamUpdated March 2026

Your home address on your PA LLC filing is a mistake you haven’t been charged for yet. When you list a personal address as your registered office with the PA Department of State, you’re inviting process servers, government agencies, and anyone who reads your public filing to show up at your door. Pennsylvania created a better solution: the Commercial Registered Office Provider.

This guide explains exactly what a CROP is, why Pennsylvania uses this term instead of “registered agent,” how to find and verify a licensed CROP, and what to look for when choosing one. If you need the short answer: a CROP is a licensed business that serves as your official Pennsylvania registered office address, so you don’t have to.

What Exactly Is a Commercial Registered Office Provider?

Under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109, every Pennsylvania business entity must maintain a registered office — a physical street address in Pennsylvania where the entity can receive legal and government documents during normal business hours. That address must appear in the entity’s public record with the Department of State.

A Commercial Registered Office Provider (CROP) is a business licensed by the PA Department of State to serve as that registered office on behalf of its clients. Instead of listing your home address or a UPS Store mailbox, you list your CROP’s commercial address. Your CROP receives all mail, scans it same-day, and notifies you immediately through a secure client portal.

This is functionally identical to what most states call a “registered agent” service. Pennsylvania simply uses different terminology under its own statutory framework. The obligations and protections are the same.

Why Pennsylvania Created the CROP License

Most states allow any adult with a physical address in the state to serve as a registered agent for a business. Pennsylvania went further. The CROP license creates a formal certification process: providers must file a Statement of Commercial Registered Office (DSCB:15-109) with the Bureau of Corporations, pay the $70 registration fee, and maintain a qualifying commercial address. Check whether your current registered office qualifies.

The result is a verifiable registry of licensed providers. Any business owner can search file.dos.pa.gov and confirm whether a provider is actually licensed at the state level — giving you an extra layer of accountability.

What a CROP Does (and Doesn’t Do)

A CROP’s primary obligations are straightforward:

  • Receive and forward legal process. If your business is sued, papers are served at the CROP’s address. The CROP scans and delivers them to you same-day. Missing a service of process can result in a default judgment — which is why the CROP’s physical availability during business hours is critical.
  • Receive government correspondence. The PA Department of State, IRS, PA Department of Revenue, and other agencies send official notices to your registered office address. Your CROP receives these and uploads them to your portal immediately.
  • Maintain your registered office listing. Your CROP’s address appears on your public filing with the PA DOS. It must be a real, staffed commercial address — not a PO box, not a virtual mailbox, not an unmanned storage location.

A CROP is not a registered agent for service of process in all contexts — for certain court filings, you may still need a designated agent. Your CROP also does not provide legal advice, act as your attorney, or manage your internal business compliance beyond the registered office function.

How to Verify a CROP Is Licensed

Before hiring any CROP, confirm they are actually licensed with the PA Department of State. The process takes two minutes:

  1. Go to file.dos.pa.gov
  2. Select “Business Entity Search”
  3. Search by the CROP’s business name
  4. Look for a filed “Statement of Commercial Registered Office” (DSCB:15-109) in their filing history

If you cannot find this filing, the provider is not licensed as a PA CROP. Using an unlicensed provider creates a gap in your registered office compliance.

What to Look for in a CROP Provider

Beyond the basic license, evaluate CROP providers on these criteria:

  • Same-day document scanning. A CROP that batches scans weekly is a liability for time-sensitive legal documents.
  • Secure digital portal. You should be able to view every document received at any time, from anywhere.
  • Annual report reminder system. The PA DOS relies on CROPs to notify clients about annual report deadlines. Confirm your CROP has an automated reminder sequence.
  • Physical commercial address. Verify the address is a real commercial location, not a virtual office service or PO box.
  • Transparent team credentials. For a legal compliance service, accountability matters. Look for a provider with verifiable licensing, a real commercial address, and a track record you can verify on file.dos.pa.gov.

How to Switch to a PA CROP

If you currently use a home address or a national registered agent and want to switch to a licensed PA CROP, the process is simple and inexpensive. File DSCB:15-108 (Statement of Change of Registered Office) with the PA DOS at file.dos.pa.gov. The fee is $5. Processing takes 7–10 business days. Frequently Asked Questions

How many licensed CROPs are there in Pennsylvania?▼
As of 2026, approximately 65 entities are licensed as Commercial Registered Office Providers with the PA Department of State. This is a very small number relative to the 3.8 million registered business entities in the state.
How do I verify a CROP is licensed?▼
Visit file.dos.pa.gov and search for the CROP's business name. Licensed CROPs have filed a Statement of Commercial Registered Office (DSCB:15-109) with the Bureau of Corporations. You can also call the Bureau at (717) 787-1057.
Is a CROP required by law?▼
You are not required to use a CROP. You may use your own physical office address as your registered office, as long as it is a real street address in Pennsylvania where someone is available during business hours to accept legal process. Many business owners prefer a CROP to protect their home address and ensure consistent availability.
What happens to my mail when I use a CROP?▼
Your CROP receives all official government correspondence and legal documents at their address. They scan each document on the day of receipt and upload it to your client portal. You receive an immediate email notification. For service of process (lawsuits), you receive notification the same day.
Can a CROP provide notarization services?▼
Some CROPs, including PA CROP Services, offer notarization as an add-on service. Our team includes a licensed Pennsylvania Notary Public.
