CROP vs. Registered Agent — Pennsylvania’s Unique Compliance System

PA CROP Services Compliance TeamUpdated March 2026

Most states use “registered agent.” Pennsylvania uses “Commercial Registered Office Provider.” If you’ve been paying a national registered agent service $200/year and wondering whether you need a PA CROP instead, the answer depends on what you’re actually getting — and what you might be missing.

The Terminology Difference

In 49 states, businesses must maintain a “registered agent” — a person or company designated to receive legal and government documents. Pennsylvania uses different statutory language. Under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109, every PA entity must maintain a “registered office” — a physical address in Pennsylvania. A “Commercial Registered Office Provider” is a licensed entity that serves as that address for multiple clients.

The functional result is the same: a reliable address where courts, government agencies, and creditors can reach your business. But the licensing framework matters in ways that affect who you should hire.

When a National RA Is Sufficient

If your PA entity is one of dozens you manage across multiple states and you want a single vendor relationship, a national RA may be appropriate. National providers offer established operations with proven procedures. For pure registered address compliance, they meet the minimum legal requirement.

When a PA CROP Is the Better Choice

A PA CROP makes more sense when:

  • Your business operates primarily in Pennsylvania and you want local expertise
  • You value same-day document handling at a real physical office (not a national sorting facility)
  • You want annual report filing included — not as a $150 add-on
  • You need help navigating PA DOS processes specifically (DSCB forms, PA entity searches, PA-specific statutes)
  • You want the 2027 deadline managed proactively by someone who understands it in depth

For most Pennsylvania small businesses, the combination of PA-specific expertise, local presence, and competitive pricing makes a licensed CROP the practical choice. Check your current compliance status to see if your registered office arrangement is working for you.

How to Switch from a National RA to PA CROP Services

Switching is straightforward. File DSCB:15-108 (Statement of Change of Registered Office) with the PA DOS at file.dos.pa.gov, listing PA CROP Services’ address (924 W 23rd St, Erie, PA 16502). The $5 fee is processed in 7–10 business days. Cancel your national RA service after the change is confirmed. See the complete change-of-registered-office guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why doesn't Pennsylvania use the term 'registered agent'?▼
Pennsylvania's Consolidated Statutes use the term 'registered office' instead of 'registered agent.' The Commercial Registered Office Provider (CROP) framework under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109 creates a state-licensed category of provider, which is more rigorous than the generic registered agent framework used in most states.
Can a national registered agent serve as my PA registered office?▼
Yes, national registered agents can provide a PA address as your registered office. PA CROPs hold a specific state license under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109. The practical difference is PA-specific expertise, local physical presence, and the CROP-specific notification obligations under PA law.
Are national registered agents licensed PA CROPs?▼
National registered agent providers operate under a different framework than Pennsylvania’s CROP licensing system. PA CROPs hold a state-level license filed with the PA Department of State. PA CROP Services is a licensed CROP under 15 Pa. C.S. § 109.
What does it cost to switch from a national RA to PA CROP Services?▼
There is no fee to cancel your national RA service (check your contract). The PA DOS change-of-registered-office fee is $5 (DSCB:15-108). PA CROP Services plans start at $99/year.
